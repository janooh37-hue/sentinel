📱 Install the App Certificate — Phone

GSSG Manager · one-time setup (about 2 minutes)

To open the app at https://gssgit.local on your phone, you install a small security certificate once. This is safe — it just tells your phone to trust our office server. You only do this a single time per device.

First: get the file gssg-ca.crt onto your phone — the easiest ways are AirDrop (iPhone), or email it to yourself and open it on the phone, or download it from a shared folder.

🍎 iPhone / iPad

  1. Get the file gssg-ca.crt onto the phone (AirDrop or email), then tap it. You'll see a message: "Profile Downloaded".
  2. Open Settings → General → VPN & Device Management.
  3. Under Downloaded Profile, tap the profile named "Caddy Local Authority".
  4. Tap Install (top-right) → enter your phone passcode → tap Install again to confirm.
  5. Now the important extra step: go to Settings → General → About → Certificate Trust Settings.
  6. Turn ON the switch next to "Caddy Local Authority", then tap Continue.
  7. Open Safari and go to https://gssgit.local. ✓ The app loads with a padlock and no warning.
⚠️ Don't skip step 5–6. On iPhone the certificate is not trusted until you turn on the switch under Certificate Trust Settings. Without it, Safari will still show "Not Secure".

🤖 Android

  1. Get the file gssg-ca.crt onto the phone (email it to yourself, USB cable, or download).
  2. Open Settings and search for "CA certificate" (or go to Security → More security settings → Encryption & credentials).
  3. Tap Install a certificate → CA certificate.
  4. If a warning appears, tap Install anyway, then browse to and select gssg-ca.crt.
  5. You'll see "Certificate installed" / "CA certificate is installed".
  6. Open Chrome and go to https://gssgit.local. ✓ The app loads with a padlock.
Menus look different? Android wording varies by brand (Samsung, Xiaomi, Oppo…). Just open Settings and search "CA certificate" — that always finds the right screen.

❓ If it still doesn't open

  1. Make sure the phone is on the same Wi-Fi as the office (not mobile data, not a guest network).
  2. Type the address exactly: https://gssgit.local (with the https).
  3. Still stuck? Try https://gssgit.local/ (with a slash), or ask IT to confirm the certificate shows as "Caddy Local Authority" and is trusted.
